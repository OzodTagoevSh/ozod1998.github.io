My favourite top movies

Captain America

Marvel's "Captain America: Civil War" finds Steve Rogers leading the newly formed team of Avengers in their continued efforts to safeguard humanity. But after another incident involving the Avengers results in collateral damage, political pressure mounts to install a system of accountability, headed by a governing body to oversee and direct the team. The new status quo fractures the Avengers, resulting in two camps-one led by Steve Rogers and his desire for the Avengers to remain free to defend humanity without government interference, and the other following Tony Stark's surprising decision to support government oversight and accountability.

  • Genre: Action and Adventure, Science Fiction and Fantasy

  • Directed By: Anthony Russo, Joe Russo

  • Written By: Christopher Markus, Stephen McFeely

  • In Theaters: May 6, 2016 Wide

  • Box Office: $408,080,554

  • Runtime: 146 minutes

  • Studio: Walt Disney Pictures


Blade Runner 2049

Thirty years after the events of the first film, a new blade runner, LAPD Officer K (Ryan Gosling), unearths a long-buried secret that has the potential to plunge what's left of society into chaos. K's discovery leads him on a quest to find Rick Deckard (Harrison Ford), a former LAPD blade runner who has been missing for 30 years.

  • Genre: Action and Adventure, Drama, Science Fiction and Fantasy

  • Directed By: Denis Villeneuve

  • Written By: Michael Green, Hampton Fancher

  • In Theaters: Oct 5, 2017 Limited

  • Box Office: $88,001,297

  • Runtime: 164 minutes

  • Studio: Warner Bros. Pictures


X-men: Days of Future Past

The ultimate X-Men ensemble fights a war for the survival of the species across two time periods in X-MEN: DAYS OF FUTURE PAST. The beloved characters from the original "X-Men" film trilogy join forces with their younger selves from the past, "X-Men: First Class," in order to change a major historical event and fight in an epic battle that could save our future.

  • Genre: Action and Adventure, Science Fiction and Fantasy

  • Directed By: Bryan Singer

  • Written By: Jane Goldman, Matthew Vaughn, Simon Kinberg

  • In Theaters: May 23, 2014 Wide

  • Box Office: $199,305,306

  • Runtime: 131 minutes

  • Studio: 20th Century Fox


Mad Max: Fury Road

Filmmaker George Miller gears up for another post-apocalyptic action adventure with Fury Road, the fourth outing in the Mad Max film series. Charlize Theron stars alongside Tom Hardy (Bronson), with Zoe Kravitz, Adelaide Clemens, and Rosie Huntington Whiteley heading up the supporting cast.

  • Genre: Action and Adventure, Science Fiction and Fantasy

  • Directed By: George Miller

  • Written By: Brendan McCarthy, Nick Lathouris, George Miller

  • In Theaters: May 15, 2015 Wide

  • Box Office: $129,483,877

  • Runtime: 120 minutes

  • Studio: Warner Bros


Skyfall

In Skyfall, Bond's loyalty to M is tested as her past comes back to haunt her. As MI6 comes under attack, 007 must track down and destroy the threat, no matter how personal the cost.

  • Genre: Action and Adventure, Mystery and Suspense

  • Directed By: Sam Mendes

  • Written By: John Logan, Robert Wade, Neal Purvis

  • In Theaters: Nov 9, 2012 Wide

  • Box Office: $299,300,000

  • Runtime: 145 minutes

  • Studio: MGM


Mission: Impossible Rogue Nation

With the IMF disbanded, and Ethan (Tom Cruise) out in the cold, the team now faces off against a network of highly skilled special agents, the Syndicate. These highly trained operatives are hellbent on creating a new world order through an escalating series of terrorist attacks. Ethan gathers his team and joins forces with disavowed British agent Ilsa Faust (Rebecca Ferguson), who may or may not be a member of this rogue nation, as the group faces their most impossible mission yet.

  • Genre: Action and Adventure

  • Directed By: Christopher McQuarrie

  • Written By: Christopher McQuarrie, Bruce Geller

  • In Theaters: Jul 31, 2015 Limited

  • Box Office: $154,979,949

  • Runtime: 131 minutes

  • Studio: Paramount Pictures
